1                              Introduction

1.1                        Purpose

The objective of this document is to establish a standard and an agreed approach towards the data backup and restoration activities perform in R______.  It is developed to ensure data availability and to reduce the security and operational risks.

1.2                        Scope

This P_____ is applicable for all R_______ International Asia branches and representative offices including RISB.

 

This document is mandatory to be applied in all offices that falls within the purview of ASIA management.  It is developed with reference from the following baseline and standards;

 

-      Operational Security Handbook; Pt. 1 Operational Security Baseline

-      AIC Risk Management Handbook; Pt. 1 Technical Security Baseline

-      British Standard BS 7799-1:1999

-      British Standard BS 7799-2:1999

1.3                        Exceptions

In-event of non-compliance from this P_____, an exception should be raised prior to the system implementation. The exception should be reviewed and approved by ITI Management.

2                              Roles and Responsibilities

The Head of ITI overseeing their office(s) should ensure that they are adhering to this P_____.  The owner of this document should be contactable at all times if there are doubts and clarifications required arising from this document.  Audit should schedule review on a periodic basis to ensure IT compliance and also to ascertain the branch risk appetite, thus minimising security and operation risks.

3.1                        Backup Rationale

Backup is a process whereby information is kept and available in a form where it can be used for the purpose of business continuity, investigation or devices failure.  In some locations, regulators have specific data retention requirements that the bank is required to adhere to. 

In R_______ , all data is recommended to exist in at least two different physical locations regardless of its form of availability.

3.2                        Capacity Planning

IT departments should periodically review the requirement of data storage size.  This should be led by the local Head of ITI with the participation from system and business owners.  Any foreseeable increase or upgrade should be forecasted, reviewed and budgeted for in-advance.

3.3                        Media Storage

For backup media which need to be kept at the office, or for those that is waiting to be transferred to the offsite location, the safety of the backup media should not be neglected.  It should be kept in a locked fire-proof vault in a secure location.  The location should also not be affected by electro-magnetic radiation.

3.4                        Documentation

Procedural documentation must be in-place to support the backup and recovery of data.  It should be version control and safe-kept in at least two or more physical sites for availability purposes.

3.5                        Backup Schedule

A backup schedule should be defined by the computer operation team.  This schedule must be accompanied with an approval signatory by their department head.  The schedule should minimally contain the type of application performing the backup, its version, the name of server/machine, the security classification[1] of information being backup and the name of the application (with version) that is capable to read the data.

This schedule shall be the daily reference for the computer operation team who is responsible for the backup process and managing its results.

3.6                        Backup Requirement Based on Event or Calendar

Certain backup could be based on event or calendar days.  Such requirement should be derived from the business owner with explicit and detail documenting the request.  This document should be jointly approve and sign-off by the computer operations head and the business owner.

3.7                        Backup of Security Audit Logs

Each IT discipline should review and define the period require for the backup of security audit logs.  IT should ensure that this information are not lost or overwritten before or during the backup process, its integrity should also not be doubtful.  It is mandatory that all security audit information is available and restorable for future needs, reference or investigation used.

3.8                        Backup Error or Failure

In a situation where there are errors noted during the backup process, the operation staff should informed the responsible engineer immediately.  The engineer should investigate and rectify the problem before attempting to perform the backup again.  He should also assure that the backup is successfully performed after the rectification.  An incident report should be submitted to the head of IT. 

 

3.9                        Offsite Storage Location

The offsite storage location for the backup media should be reasonably distance from the information originating office.  The site should minimally provide fire-proof safe for safe-keeping of critical items or documents.  The location should meet at least one of the following criteria;

 

-      The offsite storage location should not fall under the same natural disaster zone as the originating office

3.10                        Data Transferring

When backup media reaches their end-of-life, they should be disposed in accordance to the prevailing policies on media disposal. If the data is still required they should be transferred to a new media.  The transferred data should be validated to ensure its integrity and availability from the transferring process.

3.11                        Backup Testing

The purpose of the testing process is to ensure that all data is available for restore within its retention time under all circumstances.

On a monthly basis, the backup media should be recalled for testing.  Those recalls for restoration test should be of different calendar intervals.  Business owner should identify and define those dates and their required sampling data for verification.

The testing of tape requirements can also be fulfilled if the backup is frequently being recall for data restoration. This could be very frequent with some systems.

3.11.1Backup Tape Testing Workflow

The testing process consists of the following steps. Once a month, the Operators will recall three tapes from off-site for integrity checks for several sample systems:

 

·        a month old backup,

·        a 6 months or a year backup and

·        a 1 years or above old backup tape

 

The samples have to include all critical systems and some samples of non-critical systems. All systems selected for the particular month need to be detailed in the Status Template (Figure 1b). On an annual basis, backup tapes of all business systems should be tested for recoverability at least once.

 

The ITI Administrators will be required to perform the restoration and data integrity checks. Logs for restoration and screen shots for evidence on data integrity are to be collected as evidence and given to the Data Centre Manager. Any failed test is to be reported to the IT risk manager and if required, escalated to the relevant risk committees.

3.12                        Backup Tape Inventory

The purpose of Tape inventory is to ensure that all tapes are accountable and available for restore within its retention time under all circumstances.

An inventory should be available and updated at all times.  The inventory should minimally detail the type of backup media, what information is backup, the date of backup, the data life-cycle, the life-span of the media, when was it last tested or restored, and the backup approach.  A copy of the inventory should be kept offsite or the backup site and the originating office.  All backup media should be labelled and easily identified with reference to the inventory.

All Tapes are bar coded before they are to be used for all Production backup.  Tapes are accounted by Operator by Online Cisco Input Manifest CISCO URL at https://login.recall.com/auth/Login before they are picked up by Cisco Recall. A Daily Activity and a Weekly Inventory List will be sent by CISCO through email.

3.12.2Backup Tape Inventory Reconciliation

An annual physical inventory reconciliation of all backup tapes is required to ensure all tapes are accounted for. ITI engineers are to conduct the annual reconciliation by sighting the physical tapes against the backup tape inventory list, document evidence of the reconciliation and findings, and submit the findings to local head of ITI and IT Risk Manager. 

IT Risk Manager is to ensure that the backup tape reconciliation is done at least once annually in a timely manner. 

3.13                        Encryption of Backup Tapes

Backup tapes which are taken out of the banks premises and stored offsite are required to be encrypted with strong encryption.

 

 

 

 

4                              Storage Environment

 

The Storage media should be kept in an appropriate and secure environment where it would not cause degradation to the media. The following controls should be put in place:

1)    Storage systems are to be monitored by an alert system which can detect and notify staff of any outages or data replication errors. 

2)    The storage architecture for critical systems and SAN should also be reviewed on an annual basis by the ITI and IS&D architecture teams. The following should be covered during the review:

a.    Identify any single points of failure and fragility in functional design and specifications. (Note: a high availability, resilient and high redundancy architecture should be established. All components in SAN should have redundancy. Multiple links and switches for all Input / Output operations between hosts, adapters, storage processors and storage arrays should be built.)

b.    Review the technical support for both in house and vendors

3)    The findings from the review and recommended action plans should be clearly documented. The review reports are to be submitted to ITI, IS&D and Business Owners of the affected systems for approvals on the action steps.

4)    All issues identified during the monitoring and review should have remedial plans developed within 1 month. Any issues, where remedial plans are not presented or if remediation is not carried out according to the remedial plan, should be reported to IT Risk Manager, who will report to the RITSC and the relevant risk management committee. 

If the backup media is managed by external service provider, they should ensure that the bank is being notified if any incident does happen, whether if there had or had not been any damage to the banks property.

Procedure must be in-placed to control the access to this media.  It can only be requested by an approved authorised team.

 

 

 

5                              Regulatory Requirements

Data retention must be in-line with the local regulatory requirements.  Regional or global system, with its set of data resides on the regional or global server and could not be independently backed up. All data should be retained accordance to the longest retention requirement.  All regulatory requirements should be identify by the business owner and approve by the head of computer operation team.

Specific local data retention duration per location must be in-line with local compliance P_____ which provides a guideline to length of electronic data retention required. Please consult your Compliance P_____ with regard to country specific regulatory requirements on electronic data retention period.
