11. Introduction

    1.1              Purpose

The purpose of the ITI P_____ for Backup and Retention is to provide a framework for all activities related to these subjects.

    1.2              Definition and Scope

Definition

Data backup is critical for a bank. The loss of data can impact on whether a bank can do business, fulfil its legal and moral responsibilities and perform its basic business functions.

Storage of backup data can be performed both onsite and offsite.

All backups are taken with the understanding that they may one day be needed for recovery. During the commissioning of a backup, the retention settings of backed up data are defined.

Scope

This P_____ is applicable for IT Infrastructure of all R_______ entities globally.

This P_____ is relevant for the backup and retention of structured and non-structured data. It defines the global rules for backup, storage of backups and retention of backup data.

    1.3              Timelines

This P_____ is valid as of 8 May 2014.

Review cycle for this P_____ is every 2 year(s).

2Terms and Concepts

Backup

 

A backup is a snapshot of data retained usually on tape, backup disk pool or Virtual Tape Library (VTL) and which represents a recovery point in time for a system or systems. A backup is distinct from a copy of data. Whereas a copy is transitory and might for example be represented by a database dump to disk, a backup is usually copied to separate media where it is given retention characteristics.

 

A backup is not a data archive containing searchable material.

A backup is not a transitory copy of data stored temporarily on a disk.

A backup is not a synchronous or asynchronous copy of data kept in step by some form of replication.

A backup are for business continuity purposes. Archiving solutions need to be discussed and defined separately.

 

 

 

3Rules of the Backup and Retention P_____

           3.1       The decision about whether to back up data contained on a server or servers must be taken by the owner of that data

Not every server or system needs to be backed up. The decision about whether to back up a server or servers must be taken by the owner of that data. This must be the business owner, with the assistance of the application support team which supports and understands that system, if assistance is required.

           3.2       Retention periods will be standardized, defined and published

Retention of data is subject to local compliance and local laws. Backups comprise one element of that retention.

 

In order to successfully manage backup retention the number of different retention models in operation will be minimized, allowing automatic checks to be performed against server groups with the same backup model.

 

Backup and retention standards will be clearly published by the Backup team and accessible to the business. IT will retain data longer than published only to cater for operational risks or preservation orders.

3.3 The business may explicitly request a business specific retention period

Under certain conditions the data retention will be business specific as the standard branch retention offerings may be unsuitable for a particular application.

Under these circumstances it is possible for a business owner, or their representative to request a different retention to that published standard offerings. This must be explicitly requested by the business owner, after which it will be recorded as an exception and the retention period adjusted accordingly. It is the responsibility of the business owner (or their delegated representative) to request any change to the standard retention periods.

 

3.4 Recertification of backups will be performed periodically

Backups will be recertified to ensure they are still as valid as when originally set up. This will normally take place every three years. The business owner or their delegated representative will confirm that existing backups are correct. This should include checking start time, retention, requirement for daily or monthly backups such that the original backup configuration remains valid.

3.5 The backup solution must be centrally monitored

The deployed backup solution must have a monitoring and reporting toolset capable of monitoring centrally for each location. If this information can only be gathered by connecting to each backed up server and checking it manually the environment is not adequately monitored and may be said to be out of control.

           3.6      Two copies of production backups must be created to protect against media failure

In order to protect the backup integrity two media copies must be taken for all Production data, regardless of the media type to which the backup is written.

 

For the avoidance of doubt, a second backup media copy is an exact point in time copy of the same data.

 

Non production backups do not need to have a second media copy.

           3.7       Production backups must be stored in more than one physical location to protect against site loss

Production backups must be stored in more than one physical location to protect against site loss. The distance between sites is location dependent, and also based on the regulatory requirements, but must at least be in physically distinct buildings and should normally be several kilometres apart to protect against both locations being impacted by the same natural disaster. E.g. Flood, fire, earthquake.

 

Non-production backups do not need to be copied to two separate locations and may reside in the same location as the primary data.

           3.8       Off-site storage arrangements must be periodically assessed

Third party companies specialising in tape - media handling may be used for both tape transport and tape storage of an off-site copy. In all cases tapes must be transported securely. A formal contract must exist defining service levels, including recovery SLAs.

 

Security of the 3rd party must be confirmed in accordance with the Procurement P_____ of R_______ . Off-site arrangements will be periodically assessed (at least annually). Any 3rd party facility should be visited and inspected by a member of the R_______ Backup team who should confirm the facility is both secure and our media is being well managed at least every 3 years.

 

           3.9       Storing of backup Media must be secure

Within a disk based backup target, backups will always be stored within the array itself.

 

Within a physical tape library backups are stored on transportable media i.e. tape cartridges. As such, tape libraries must be located in a secure part of the building; typically a secure data centre. Tapes containing data which are held on-site at R_______ will be held within tape libraries or within a secure cabinet.

 

Tapes held within the tape libraries will be catalogued by the backup tool. Where tapes containing active data are held outside the tape library a tape inventory must be maintained by IT, or by any third party tape handling company.

            3.10      Data protection measures should be considered where backup data is physically sent off-site

Protection of data on tape media must be considered where backup data is physically sent off-site. The decision on whether to protect data on tapes should be taken locally, depending upon local security factors and assessment of the mechanism of off-siting of data. Data on media does not need to be protected if media is held on RI premises.

            3.11      Procedures for the backup & restore of data must exist

Procedures for backup systems must be documented and implemented. Documentation will be available across production and BCP sites.

 

 

 

      3.12       Performance must be measured for effectiveness

The performance of a backup solution against these backup principles must be measured in order to ensure the effectiveness of the process.

 

Restores should be tested as a means of verifying the robustness of a sites backup P_____. Testing will normally concentrate of high availability systems. The quality of the backups will at least be monitored by the successfulness of ad-hoc restores. In particular how long a restore takes and whether it was successful or not will be recorded.

      3.13       Legacy backup data must be managed

Backup data must continue to have its retention characteristics maintained and be available for restore regardless of changes in software, hardware or architecture.

 

4Governance

4.1            Roles & Responsibilities

The following roles should at least be accounted for within the IT organisation and have the following minimal set of responsibilities.

Role

Responsibility

Backup team

        The team responsible for managing the backup and recovery environment

Business owner

        The owner of a system or systems being backed up

Application support team

        The technical IT team managing an application or applications on behalf of the business owner

ITI team

        A functional team within ITI (e.g. Storage team)

Third party company

        An external company optionally providing a service for R_______ . In this context it might be an off-site storage company.

Compliance Department

        The compliance department for that country or entity responsible for defining compliance driven retention or destruction

Legal Department

        The legal department for that country or entity responsible for defining legal driven retention or destruction

4.2            Governance structure

Global Head of Datacentre Services, DCTS

The global head of DCTS will be responsible for all the review, maintenance and change of this P_____.

Storage CoC

The Storage CoC will be responsible for the creation and maintenance of all Backup Storage and Retention standards in accordance with this P_____.
